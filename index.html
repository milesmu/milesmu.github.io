Mental Health

Generalized Mental Health

A general definition of mental health can be summarized as a person's condition with regard to their psychological and emotional well-being. There is a myriad of different categories and types of mental illnesses that can affect a person's psychological or emotional state, including anxiety, depression, and eating disorders.

In the United States, mental illnesses are considered to be common. About 20% of adults in the U.S. live with a mental illness. The severity of a mental illness varies from person to person and depends on circumstances. Generally, these levels of severity are known as mild, moderate, or severe.

Effects of COVID on Mental Health

With COVID-19, teenagers find themselves trudging through months of remote learning isolated in their homes. Studies have found increased rates of anxiety and depression among teenagers in 2020.

Many teens have lost family members and/or close friends to COVID; others have parents working as essential workers or developing the vaccine.
